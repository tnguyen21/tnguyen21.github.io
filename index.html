About

I am a computer scientist, writer, and book lover bouncing homebody-ing in Chicago. Currently, I am pursuing a BS in Computer Science and a minor in Mathematics at Drexel University. Previously, I have worked at Vanguard as an IT Developer Intern and assistant researcher in the Drexel Wireless Systems Laboratory. I have also worked as a web developer for Rise First.

At the moment, I am a full time student doing some web development and web design on the side for the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers (SASE) Drexel Chapter. This coming fall, I will be a Software Engineer with Sharing Excess.

Looking towards the future, I'd like to broaden my skillset and become a full-stack developer with some specialized knowledge in high level statistics and machine learning. I see myself working on projects that serve a greater social good, and innovating fields that have historically been overlooked by individuals currently working in tech.

In my free time I like to read speculative fiction, tech, culture, history, and politics. I like to consider myself aggressively curious and like to pick up new hobbies and skills when I can. Every so often I'll also find time to write. I maintain a small newsletter where I talk about the things I'm reading.

Relevant Coursework

CS 375: Web and Mobile App Development

An introduction to web-based and mobile technologies. Covers basic HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and then developing web-based/mobile back-ends with Node.JS and MySQL.

CS 260: Data Structures

That imfamous class all CS majors know of. The one where we learn (hypothetically) how to solve LeetCode hards. Covers stacks, queues, linked allocation, binary trees, internal searching and sorting, hashing, and applications.

CS 283: Systems Programming

An introduction to computer systems, namely the interaction between hardware and software through the operating system. Primarily taught in C.

SE 181: Introduction to Software Engineering and Development

An introduction to software engineering principles, with a term-long team project used to demonstrate and practice good software engineering practices.

CS 270: Mathematical Foundations of Computer Science

Introduces formal logic and its connections to Computer Science.

CS 283: Systems Programming

Covers the interaction of hardware and software through the operating system, from the programmer's perspective. Topics covered include linking, system level I/O, concurrent programming, and network programming.

CS 265: Advanced Programming Techniques

An introduction to UNIX/Linux shell, bash scripting, and C.

MATH 200: Multivariate Calculus

Vectors, curves, partial derivatives, gradient, constrained optimization, coordinate system, multiple integrals, and applications.

MATH 201: Linear Algebra

Systems of linear equations, matrix algebra, determinants, vector spaces, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, orthogonality, diagonalization, applications.
